# CS 230 - Final Project - The Gamimg Room design document

### Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements. Who was the client? What type of software did they want you to design?
The Gaming Room was our client, a company that had already developed Draw It or Lose It, an Android-based game similar to the 1980s television show Win, Lose or Draw. 
They wanted us to design a web-based, multi-platform expansion of their existing game that would support Windows, Mac, Linux, and mobile devices while maintaining cross-platform compatibility. 
The software needed to support multiple concurrent games with teams and players, render stock images progressively over 30-second intervals, enforce unique naming for games and teams, and ensure only single game instances existed in memory at any time.


### What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?
Researching solutions and providing recommendations. 


### What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?
Forced me to work systematically and plan ahead in a big picture way.


### If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
I would have liked to explore the recommendations in project two more - the various pros and cons of each of the operating systems. I think I got a bit lost in the weeds. 

### How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?
I focused on the user needs while thinking ahead about what exisiting architecture may be repurposed accross multiple platforms. 
It's important to balance the user needs with performance, if the users you do have are unable to use the app reliably that will lead to lose of users. 

### How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?
I prefer test-driven developement, with a scrum 2 week sprint sessions. It's really difficult to design a product without feedback, I would have liked to work in a group.
